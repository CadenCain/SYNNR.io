# SYNNR — Self-Serve Pricing & Marketing-Page Plan

Goal: price so a shop says yes without a call, and so revenue **scales per yard** as they grow. Value metric = **the yard** (price grows with their operation, feels fair, no per-seat friction).

---

## The pricing model (self-serve, per yard / month)

| Tier | For | Price (billed annually) | Monthly | What's in it |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Single Yard** | 1 yard, getting started | **$149**/yard/mo | $179 | Unlimited assets & users, photo + proof storage, email expiration alerts, the full renewal loop |
| **Operator** ⭐ *Most popular* | Growing shops, 2+ yards | **$129**/yard/mo | $159 | Everything in Single **+ SMS "you get the text" alerts, roles/permissions, CSV import, priority support** |
| **Fleet** | Multi-yard operations | **Custom** | — | Everything + SSO, **done-for-you yard load**, custom integrations, dedicated support |

**Add-on (one-time):** *"We'll load your yard for you"* — **$499–$1,500** depending on size. Removes the data-entry wall; also your bridge from self-serve into the high-ticket custom work.

**Terms:** 14-day free trial (card required), cancel anytime, annual = **2 months free**.

### Why these numbers (the psychology)
1. **Anchor with the cost of a miss before showing price** — once they've seen "$10k NPT day," $149 reads as a rounding error.
2. **Three tiers, center-stage** — the middle "Operator" tier is marked *Most popular*; the volume price ($129 vs $149) makes it the obvious value pick. The decoy on either side makes the choice easy.
3. **Charm pricing** — $149 / $129, not $150 / $130. Reads materially cheaper.
4. **Per-yard value metric** — price scales with their growth, not arbitrary seats. They never feel punished for adding users.
5. **Annual = 2 months free** — better cash up front, higher retention, and it's a commitment device.
6. **Reframe to per-day** on the page: *"less than $5/day per yard."* Trivial-izes the spend.
7. **Risk reversal** — free trial, cancel anytime, "import your list in minutes." Kills the "what if my guys won't use it" objection.
8. **Name tiers by who, not by feature count** — reduces overload; shops self-select.

## The ROI math (put this ON the marketing page)

| What a miss costs | Typical hit |
|---|---|
| A day of NPT on location | $10,000+ |
| A hotshot to chase what got left behind | $500–$2,000 |
| A failed DOT inspection (truck sidelined) | Thousands + lost days |
| A failed safety audit | Fines, shutdowns, paperwork hell |
| Getting dropped from an operator's vendor list | The whole account |

**The line that closes it:**
> One yard runs **$149/month — about $5 a day.** One prevented NPT day covers **more than five years** of it. You're not buying software. You're buying never getting turned around at the gate again.
